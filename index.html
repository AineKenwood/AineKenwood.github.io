Experimental Presets

For this experiment, first find the settings box in the lower right section of the screen. Within "system Settings", select “Temperature and Speed” from the experimental preset drop down menu. After the settings change, vary the Temperature setting and observe how it affects the system

Consider the following questions

  • When you increase temperature, what happens to the speed of the particles? What about when you decrease temperature?
  • Can you get the particles to form a crystal structure?
  • When do you see more intermolecular interactions occurring, at high or low temperatures?
  • When do the particles form a solid? When do they form a gas?

For this experiment, select “Attractive and Repulsive” forces within the experimental preset drop down menu. After the settings change, You will now see the particles color’s change to green and red. These colors represent the balance between the attractive and repulsive forces acting on the particles. When a particle is green, it is feeling an attractive force. When a particle is red, it is a repulsive force. When attractive and repulsive forces are in equilibrium, the particle will switch between red and green rapidly.

For this experiment, select “Force and Distance” within the experimental preset drop down menu. After the settings change, click a particle and observe how its position, velocity and acceleration change depending on how far/close it is to other particles. Remember that acceleration is related to the net force a particle is experiencing by Newton’s second Law: F = ma (Net force = mass * acceleration).

For this experiment, select “Particle Size” within the experimental preset drop down menu. After the settings change, vary the radius setting and observe how the strength of interactions between particles change.
